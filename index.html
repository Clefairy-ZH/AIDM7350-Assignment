    T-POP

    Thai pop, also known as T-pop, is a type of Thai music that is roughly equivalent to western pop. It first emerged in the 1970s and 1980s as string music (Thai: ), before gaining mainstream popularity in the 1990s and dominating the Thai music industry ever since. The term is extremely broad, encompassing Thai rock, dance music, rap, and western-influenced popular music in general, excluding folk and rock-influenced phleng phuea chiwit.
